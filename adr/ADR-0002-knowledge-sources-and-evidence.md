# ADR-0002 — Knowledge Sources and Evidence

**Status:** Accepted

**Date:** 2026-07-23

---

# Context

The Engineering Excellence Playbook is intended to provide practical, original engineering guidance.

To remain credible, the playbook should be informed by recognized standards, established literature, research and real-world engineering experience. At the same time, chapters should remain readable and should not become academic papers or summaries of external sources.

A consistent policy is therefore required for how sources are selected, used and referenced.

---

# Decision

The Engineering Excellence Playbook is an original synthesis of:

- International standards.
- Academic and industry research.
- Influential engineering literature.
- Established industry practices.
- Practical engineering experience.

Major claims and models should be traceable to one or more of these source categories, either directly or conceptually.

Chapters should explain ideas in the playbook's own language and structure. External sources provide grounding and further reading; they do not replace the playbook's reasoning or practical guidance.

---

# Referencing Approach

Each major chapter should include a concise **References and Further Reading** section when relevant.

References should be selected because they:

- Support or clarify important concepts in the chapter.
- Represent recognized standards or influential works.
- Provide useful depth beyond the chapter.
- Remain relevant to professional engineering practice.

Inline citations should be used only when a specific definition, model, quotation or externally attributable claim requires precise attribution.

---

# Source Categories

## International Standards

Standards may be used to ground terminology, lifecycle processes, quality models, architecture descriptions, security practices and risk management.

## Research and Industry Evidence

Research and industry studies may be used to support claims about engineering performance, delivery, reliability and organizational outcomes.

## Engineering Literature

Books and papers may be referenced when they introduced, clarified or strongly influenced an engineering concept.

## Practical Experience

Operational experience, delivery lessons and engineering judgment may be used when clearly presented as practical guidance rather than universal fact.

---

# Guardrails

The repository will not:

- Copy or closely paraphrase protected source material.
- Present standards as universally mandatory when applicability depends on context.
- Use famous names as a substitute for reasoning.
- Add references only for appearance or authority.
- Turn chapters into literature reviews.
- Claim formal compliance without sufficient evidence.

---

# Consequences

This decision increases the review effort for major chapters because important claims and models must be checked against credible sources.

It also strengthens the playbook by combining practical clarity with recognized engineering knowledge while preserving an original and coherent voice.

---

# Guiding Question

Before adding a source, ask:

> **Does this reference materially improve the accuracy, credibility or usefulness of the chapter?**

If the answer is no, it should not be included.
